Cropped, unedited, saved for web

Part of a forthcoming RIDIN' series. A subaru with illegal NY plate heads south on 85 away from Buckhead toward Downtown. This picture captures the beauty in my truly amateur photography skills. At first, I never had a clue whether the photo would turn out well. Today, often times, I still don't know.

Cabbagetown, Atlanta is a small neighborhood. Plenty of ATL "natives" don't know it exists and/or have never been there. It's the most funky, tucked-in, and teeny tiny neighboorhood that I have ever seen. I lived in Cabbagetown for over a year and it transformed my perspective on Atlanta forever.

There was a club in Aspen called the record room or something like that. An entire long wall with multiple shelves full of vinyl and a PacMan Ghost guardian fending off all the Aspen thots.

WIKI and Antwon came through Atlanta on the We Stole Hip Hop Tour. I bought 2 tickets but nobody wanted to come with me - "classic". They both performed really well. This picture is from after WIKI finished his solo set and brought Antwon back out. WIKI wore a t shirt to rep Knowledge Wave. It's an internet radio station collective type thing that continues to make big impressions on my life

I expected Future was more fun to watch at the Tabernacle from the top of the venue, but I had to sneak up close to be sure of that. These "amateur" shots turn out really well for me. THe distorition portrays Future in a visibily super-human state. I feel lucky to have lived in Atlanta from 2013-2016. Atlanta has always been a home of hip hop for me since I discovered the genre. But, a number of artists (rappers and producers) climbed to new heights at a new pace while I lived there. I take no credit.

This Gaucho from Argentina spent his morning riding horses with my family. He took us all over these mountanis and hills. It was the first time I rode a horse. We stopped halfway at this spot up high with a tremendous view and drank mate. We shared a Marb Red when my Mom and Dad werne't looking :). My sister laughed with her teeth out. This was the first time I ever drank Mate. Mate rules. I still drink it.

When I lived in Cabbagetown I came home to a view like this every day. The adjacent container yard and MARTA track made me love my apartment in Cabbagetown even more. Often times Atlanta doesn't feel like a city to me because of the lack of constant motion that a New York offers. But, this apartment put me in a position to see traffic of all kinds 24/7. I like that vibe. When lots of city / natural sounds would happen in a sequence (do sounds happen?), my then-roommate at the time called it GOD on the 1's and 2's. Ha!

I took this after getting kicked out of the forementioned container yard. It's a picture of my apartment building and the water tower sat right outside the windows to the main room. An angel climbed up part of that water tower once. I never touched it. The building was one of many in a massive yard that used to be a cotton mill. Fulton Cotton Mill
